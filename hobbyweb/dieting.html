Dieting Guide

Pick the nutritional framework that matches your current training goal.

Calorie Surplus
+200 – 400 kcal above TDEE

The Bodybuilding & Hypertrophy Framework

1. Core Caloric Strategy
  • The Surplus Bounds: Add 200 to 400 calories to your measured baseline daily. Aggressive surpluses exceeding 500 calories accelerate adipose (fat) tissue storage without increasing muscle protein synthesis limits.
  • Target Growth Metric: Aim for a total body weight gain of 0.5kg to 1kg per month. Faster progression points to excessive body fat accumulation.
2. Macronutrient Optimization
  • Protein Formulation (2.2g per kg): Provides a constant pool of floating amino acids. Focus strictly on complete proteins containing all nine essential amino acids.
  • Carbohydrates Formulation (4.0g – 6.0g per kg): Fuels high-volume training cycles and keeps muscle structures fully filled via structural glycogen integration.
  • Fats Formulation (0.8g – 1.0g per kg): Maintains fundamental hormone production baseline settings (testosterone creation tracks directly with healthy fat intake updates).
3. Meal Timing & Muscle Protein Synthesis (MPS)
  • Dosing Frequency: Break up total daily macro targets into 4 to 5 distinct meals spaced 3 to 4 hours apart. Each block must clear a 30g to 40g threshold to trigger active muscle repair sequences.
  • Pre-Workout Loading: Consume 30g of fast-digesting carbohydrates balanced alongside 30g of stable protein 60 to 90 minutes before stepping onto the gym floor.
  • Post-Workout Anabolic Capture: Consume a highly bioavailable protein option (such as whey isolates) within 60 minutes of training to clear systemic cell breakdown and switch on repair states.
Performance & Recovery
Maintenance or +100 – 200 kcal

The Power & Strength Framework

1. Core Caloric Strategy
  • The Baseline Rule: Operate directly at localized maintenance caloric numbers or within a controlled micro-surplus of 100 to 200 calories max.
  • Deficit Warnings: Avoid run-state caloric deficits while working through structural strength blocks. Depleting absolute structural reserves scales down your neurological output potential and directly alters baseline lift safety mechanics.
2. Macronutrient Optimization
  • Protein Formulation (1.6g – 2.0g per kg): Requires slightly less total daily protein volume allocations than pure bodybuilding profiles, due to lowered cumulative mechanical breakdown structures across low-rep strength targets.
  • Carbohydrates Formulation (3.5g – 5.0g per kg): Mandated to fuel phosphagen energy pathways and key glycolytic systems triggered across intense heavy compound movements.
  • Fats Formulation (1.0g – 1.2g per kg): Higher relative parameters optimize compound joint function pathways and systemic inflammation regulation. Focus on deep omega-3 sources (salmon, clean fish oils) to preserve tissue parameters.
3. Intra-Workout Fuelling & Electrolyte Management
  • The Sodium Matrix: Intense force generation needs high intra-muscular pressure profiles. Aim for 500mg to 1000mg of structural sodium near training frames to support hydration parameters and optimize lifting leverage.
  • Intra-Workout Carbs: When passing the 90-minute point of training, sip on 30g of functional branched cyclic dextrin formulas to avoid central fatigue states.
  • Post-Workout System Backing: Balance immediate protein inputs alongside high micronutrient-dense items (dark berries, dense greens) to neutralize system strain elements.

Cutting / Fat Loss

The Ultimate Low-Calorie Shred Bowl

420 kcal 52g protein 8g fat 35g carbs

Ingredients

  • 180g NTUC Pasar chicken breast
  • 150g cauliflower rice
  • 100g broccoli
  • 10g light soy sauce
  • 5ml sesame oil

Method

  1. Dice the chicken breast into even cubes and season with salt, pepper, and light soy sauce.
  2. Heat the sesame oil in a non-stick pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Pan-sear the chicken for 6 – 8 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 75°C.
  4. Steam the cauliflower rice and broccoli separately, then combine into a bowl with the chicken.

Budget / Clean Bulk

High-Protein Budget Egg & Tofu Scramble

580 kcal 45g protein 22g fat 50g carbs

Ingredients

  • 1 block Fortune pressed tofu (tau kwa)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 100g jasmine rice
  • Spring onions, to taste

Method

  1. Mash the tofu block using a fork until it crumbles into egg-like pieces.
  2. Whisk the 3 eggs in a bowl with a pinch of salt.
  3. Pour the mashed tofu into a hot pan and cook for 3 minutes to remove excess moisture.
  4. Add the whisked eggs directly into the pan, stirring continuously until cooked, then serve over hot jasmine rice.
