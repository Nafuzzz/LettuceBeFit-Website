Exercise
Incline Dumbbell Curl

Why: Sitting on an incline places the bicep's long head into extreme stretch, increasing time under tension and maximising muscle fibre recruitment.

How: Set a bench to a 45-degree incline. Sit back, let your arms hang straight down, keep your elbows pinned. Curl the weights up without swinging your shoulders.

Exercise
Weighted Chin-Up

Why: A heavy compound movement that allows you to load the biceps with significantly more absolute weight than any isolation curl can provide.

How: Wear a dip belt with weight plates. Grip the pull-up bar with an underhand, shoulder-width grip. Pull yourself up until your chin clears the bar, then lower with control.

Exercise
Overhead Cable Tricep Extension

Why: Places the long head of the tricep (the largest section) into a fully stretched position overhead, driving hypertrophy.

How: Attach a rope to a cable at chest height. Face away, hold rope behind head, lean forward, and extend elbows straight out in front of you.

Exercise
Close-Grip Barbell Bench Press

Why: Narrowing your grip shifts the load from your chest to your triceps, allowing you to press heavy loads safely.

How: Grip the bar at shoulder-width. Lower to your lower chest keeping elbows tucked to your sides, then press up.

Exercise
Wide-Grip Lat Pulldown

Why: Targets the vertical fibres of the latissimus dorsi, widening the upper back to create a distinct V-taper appearance.

How: Grip the bar wider than shoulder-width. Pull your shoulder blades down and drive your elbows toward your hips, bringing the bar to your upper chest.

Exercise
Conventional Barbell Deadlift

Why: The definitive posterior chain movement. Builds structural strength from your hamstrings through your spinal erectors and upper traps.

How: Stand with mid-foot under the bar. Hinge down, grip the bar, flatten your back, and push the floor away with your legs to stand with the weight.

Exercise
Dumbbell Lateral Raise

Why: Directly isolates the lateral deltoid head, expanding the width of your frame and building shoulder roundness.

How: Stand holding dumbbells at your sides. Lean slightly forward and raise your arms out to your sides along the scapular plane (roughly 30 degrees forward) until shoulder height.

Exercise
Standing Overhead Barbell Press (OHP)

Why: Requires total body stabilisation, loading your front deltoids, triceps, core, and upper back simultaneously.

How: Clean the bar to your upper chest. Squeeze glutes, brace your core, and press straight up overhead — move your face back slightly to let the bar clear.

Exercise
Hack Squat Machine

Why: Stabilises your torso and eliminates balance requirements so your nervous system can safely push your quadriceps to absolute muscular failure.

How: Position your back flat against the pad, feet low on the platform. Release safety catches, lower until thighs break parallel, then drive back up.

Add exercise image or video here
Exercise
Barbell Back Squat

Why: Forces the quadriceps, glutes, adductors, and core to work together as a single unit to move maximum weight — the foundation of lower body power.

How: Rest the bar across your upper traps, feet shoulder-width apart. Push hips back and lower until your hip crease drops below your knees, then drive up hard.
