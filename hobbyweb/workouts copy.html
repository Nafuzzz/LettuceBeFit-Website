Muscle Group 01

Biceps

GoalExercises / SessionSetsTarget RepsRest
Bodybuilding (Hypertrophy)2 – 33 – 48 – 1260 – 90 sec

Exercise 1
Incline Dumbbell Curl

Why: Sitting on an incline places the bicep's long head into extreme stretch, increasing time under tension and maximising muscle fibre recruitment.

How: Set a bench to a 45-degree incline. Sit back, let your arms hang straight down, keep your elbows pinned. Curl the weights up without swinging your shoulders.

Exercise 2
Preacher Curl

Why: The arm pad locks your elbows in place, eliminating momentum and isolating the short head for better inner-bicep thickness.

How: Sit at a preacher bench, rest your upper arms flat against the pad. Lower the barbell fully until your arms extend, then curl it up — stopping just before your forearms reach vertical.

Exercise 1
Weighted Chin-Up

Why: A heavy compound movement that allows you to load the biceps with significantly more absolute weight than any isolation curl can provide.

How: Wear a dip belt with weight plates. Grip the pull-up bar with an underhand, shoulder-width grip. Pull yourself up until your chin clears the bar, then lower with control.

Exercise 2
Heavy Barbell Curl

Why: Allows for maximum mechanical tension and progressive overload across the entire biceps brachii complex.

How: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, hold a barbell underhand. Squeeze glutes and core to prevent swinging, then curl the bar toward your chest.

Triceps

Exercise 1
Overhead Cable Tricep Extension

Why: Places the long head of the tricep (the largest section) into a fully stretched position overhead, driving hypertrophy.

How: Attach a rope to a cable at chest height. Face away, hold rope behind head, lean forward, and extend elbows straight out in front of you.

Exercise 2
Cable Tricep Pushdown

Why: Cables provide constant tension throughout the entire range of motion, isolating the lateral and medial heads of the triceps.

How: Face the cable machine with a straight bar or rope. Tuck elbows tightly against your ribs and push the weight down until your arms lock out completely.

Exercise 1
Close-Grip Barbell Bench Press

Why: Narrowing your grip shifts the load from your chest to your triceps, allowing you to press heavy loads safely.

How: Grip the bar at shoulder-width. Lower to your lower chest keeping elbows tucked to your sides, then press up.

Exercise 2
Weighted Dip

Why: Forces your triceps to manage your bodyweight plus added resistance, creating high mechanical tension.

How: Grip parallel dip bars. Keep your torso upright to focus on the triceps. Lower until your shoulders drop just below your elbows, then drive back up.

Back

Exercise 1
Wide-Grip Lat Pulldown

Why: Targets the vertical fibres of the latissimus dorsi, widening the upper back to create a distinct V-taper appearance.

How: Grip the bar wider than shoulder-width. Pull your shoulder blades down and drive your elbows toward your hips, bringing the bar to your upper chest.

Exercise 2
Chest-Supported T-Bar Row

Why: Supporting your chest removes lower back fatigue entirely, letting you isolate and fully exhaust the mid-back, rhomboids, and traps.

How: Lie face down on the pad. Pull the weight toward your torso, squeeze shoulder blades together at the top, and lower slowly.

Exercise 1
Conventional Barbell Deadlift

Why: The definitive posterior chain movement. Builds structural strength from your hamstrings through your spinal erectors and upper traps.

How: Stand with mid-foot under the bar. Hinge down, grip the bar, flatten your back, and push the floor away with your legs to stand with the weight.

Exercise 2
Barbell Row

Why: Develops explosive horizontal pulling power and absolute density across the entire upper and mid-back.

How: Hinge forward at 45 degrees, overhand grip. Pull the bar dynamically to your lower stomach, keeping your spine completely neutral.

Muscle Group 04

Shoulders

GoalExercises / SessionSetsTarget RepsRest
Bodybuilding (Hypertrophy)33 – 410 – 1560 – 90 sec
Power & Strength1 – 24 – 54 – 62 – 3 min
Exercise 1
Dumbbell Lateral Raise

Why: Directly isolates the lateral deltoid head, expanding the width of your frame and building shoulder roundness.

How: Stand holding dumbbells at your sides. Lean slightly forward and raise your arms out to your sides along the scapular plane (roughly 30 degrees forward) until shoulder height.

Exercise 2
Reverse Dumbbell Fly (Rear Delts)

Why: Balances the shoulder joint and creates a full 3-D shoulder profile — most lifters neglect this entirely.

How: Hinge forward until your torso is nearly parallel to the floor. With a slight elbow bend, raise the dumbbells out horizontally, leading with your elbows.

Exercise 1
Standing Overhead Barbell Press (OHP)

Why: Requires total body stabilisation, loading your front deltoids, triceps, core, and upper back simultaneously.

How: Clean the bar to your upper chest. Squeeze glutes, brace your core, and press straight up overhead — move your face back slightly to let the bar clear.

Exercise 2
Push Press

Why: A slight leg drive bypasses the initial sticking point, allowing you to overload your shoulders with 10–20% more weight than a strict press.

How: Set up like the OHP. Dip your knees slightly (2–3 inches), then drive upward aggressively through your legs to press the bar overhead.

Muscle Group 05

Legs

GoalExercises / SessionSetsTarget RepsRest
Bodybuilding (Hypertrophy)3 – 43 – 48 – 1290 – 120 sec
Power & Strength24 – 63 – 53 min
Exercise 1
Hack Squat Machine

Why: Stabilises your torso and eliminates balance requirements so your nervous system can safely push your quadriceps to absolute muscular failure.

How: Position your back flat against the pad, feet low on the platform. Release safety catches, lower until thighs break parallel, then drive back up.

Exercise 2
Lying Leg Curl

Why: Provides isolated mechanical tension on the hamstrings via knee flexion — which cannot be fully replicated by hip hinge movements alone.

How: Lie face down, align your knees with the pivot point. Pull the roller pad toward your glutes quickly, hold briefly, then lower under control.

Exercise 1
Barbell Back Squat

Why: Forces the quadriceps, glutes, adductors, and core to work together as a single unit to move maximum weight — the foundation of lower body power.

How: Rest the bar across your upper traps, feet shoulder-width apart. Push hips back and lower until your hip crease drops below your knees, then drive up hard.

Exercise 2
Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

Why: Maximises eccentric loading and pure pulling strength across the glutes, hamstrings, and lower back — protecting you against injury.

How: Hold a barbell at your hips, knees slightly bent and fixed. Push your hips back as far as possible, lowering the bar down your thighs until a deep hamstring stretch, then return to standing.
